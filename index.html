Analysis of Algorithms

The goal of analyzing an algorithm is to provide a general baseline for its efficiency, as measured by the time and space needed to finish its execution. To do this, we first need to specify an appropriate model of computation. We want our hypothetical model to be general enough so that our analysis remains true regardless of the device it is being executed on, and easy enough to reason with. The Random-Access Machine (RAM) model satisfies these requirements.
